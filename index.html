TOP things to do in Pitigliano

Take a Day Trip to Porto Santo Stefano!

Escape the hustle and bustle of city life with a day trip to the charming coastal town of Porto Santo Stefano. Bask in the warm Mediterranean sun as you stroll along the picturesque waterfront, lined with colorful fishing boats and waterfront cafes. Dip your toes in the crystal-clear waters of the Tyrrhenian Sea, or embark on a scenic boat tour of the rugged coastline and hidden coves. Indulge in freshly caught seafood at a seaside trattoria, and soak up the relaxed ambiance of this idyllic seaside retreat.

Explore Wineries near Pitigliano

Indulge your senses in the flavors of Tuscany with a visit to a local vineyard, where the art of winemaking has been perfected over generations. Sample a selection of fine wines, from crisp whites to full-bodied reds, as you learn about the unique terroir and varietals that make Tuscan wine world-renowned. Savor the complexities of each vintage as you sip and swirl, and discover the passion and craftsmanship that go into every bottle.

Take a Guided Tour of the Vie Cave Etruscan Roads!

Embark on a journey of discovery along the ancient Vie Cave, a network of sunken roads carved by the Etruscans over two millennia ago. Follow in the footsteps of ancient travelers as you walk along these mysterious pathways, marveling at the ingenuity of their engineering and the beauty of their natural surroundings. Learn about the history and significance of the Vie Cave from expert guides, who will illuminate the secrets of this fascinating archaeological wonder.

Visit the Orsini Fortress for Panoramic Views!

Ascend to the lofty heights of the Orsini Fortress and be rewarded with sweeping vistas of Pitigliano and the surrounding countryside. Marvel at the formidable stone walls and imposing battlements of this medieval stronghold, as you soak in the commanding views from its strategic perch atop a rocky promontory. Capture the beauty of the landscape with your camera, and savor the moment as you gaze out over the timeless beauty of Tuscany.

Discover the Etruscan Tombs of Sovana

Step back in time to the era of the Etruscans with a visit to the ancient necropolis of Sovana, a town near Pitigliano. Marvel at the intricately carved tombs hewn from the rocky landscape, adorned with elaborate frescoes and inscriptions that offer glimpses into Etruscan customs and beliefs. Walk from Pitigliano to Sovana in about 2 hours (8 km) and take the bus back. Best Program near Pitigliano with kids.

Relax in the Thermal Baths of Saturnia!

Treat yourself to a day of relaxation and rejuvenation at the legendary thermal baths of Saturnia. Bask in the warm embrace of natural hot springs, renowned for their therapeutic properties and healing mineral waters. Let your cares melt away as you soak in the soothing warmth, surrounded by lush greenery and cascading waterfalls, for a blissful escape from the stresses of modern life.

Take a scenic drive through the Tuscan Countryside!

Embark on a leisurely drive through the rolling hills and picturesque valleys of the Tuscan countryside, where every bend in the road reveals breathtaking vistas of vineyards, olive groves, and ancient hilltop villages. Stop at roadside viewpoints to capture postcard-worthy panoramas, and immerse yourself in the timeless beauty of rural Italy.

Explore the historic streets of the Old Town!

Lose yourself in the enchanting labyrinth of Pitigliano's Old Town, where every cobblestone street whispers tales of centuries past. Admire the well-preserved medieval architecture, browse artisan shops for unique treasures, and savor the authentic flavors of Tuscan cuisine in cozy trattorias tucked away in hidden corners.

How to get there?

Airplane

Nearest international airport is in Rome (150 km), an other option is the international airport of Florence (200 km). We recommend to rent a car at the airport.

Check Flights!

Train

Reaching Pitigliano by public transport is very difficult and takes a lot of time as it is not located along the major railway lines. From Rome it takes almost 6 hours to get there by train and bus.

Car

From Rome: 150 km, approx. 2h

From Florence: 200 km, 2h 40min
